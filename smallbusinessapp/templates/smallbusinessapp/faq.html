Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What is the purpose of this website?
    While public companies are required to report their revenue every quarter, private businesses have no such obligation, making it hard to know the size of a private company. We have used novel freely available, public data sources to create models of company revenue, payroll, and number of employees.
  2. What businesses can be found in the database?
    We have over 10 million small businesses accessible in the database. A small business is defined as one that has less than 500 employees. If you want to know if we have data on a business, just look it up and see.
  3. Where does your data come from?
    We have obtained novel data sources from the US Government using the Freedom of Information Act. Our Revenue and Payroll estimates are modeled from this data and the employee numbers come from data directly reported from the business to the Government.
  4. How do you model revenue?
    We use a machine learning model that uses company payroll and employee data and specific industry productivity rates from the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) to estimate a company's revenue. We make no claim about the accuracy of our modeled revenue metrics and they are to be used at your own discretion.
  5. How do you model payroll?
    We use a model based on the reported data to the Government. For businesses that have a lot of high paid ($100,000+) employees, the payroll estimates will probably be lower. We make no claim about the accuracy of our modeled payroll metrics and they are to be used at your own discretion.
  6. How do you get your number of employees data?
    This data comes directly from information businesses reported to the Government when receiving a loan. The data was reported about 1-2 years ago.
